## ⚠️ Flags to resolve with partners

1. **The Shed — address + visit model.** No public address found online. Need coordinates from the owner, and whether students can drop in (geofence check-in) or only visit during scheduled tours (make it an event-day or time-windowed challenge).
2. **Tuskegee Honey — where does a check-in happen?** Small apiary; if there's no visitable location, best model is co-locating their challenge at the Macon County Farmers Market ("find Tuskegee Honey's stand") or at the pilot event itself.
3. **Shady Grove — picking season vs. pilot date.** U-pick runs late May–late July; an early-August pilot may just miss it. Options: schedule their challenge as the season finale weekend before the event, or a non-picking farm visit. Also: it's on CR 81 outside town — car required; set a generous geofence radius.
4. **Trail balance.** Arts has one location and Food has two. Recommendation: add 2–3 **free outdoor campus landmarks** that need no partner participation — Booker T. Washington Monument "Lifting the Veil" (History), Tuskegee University Chapel exterior (Arts) — as low-point (25 pt) check-ins. This gives every trail ≥2 stops, gives freshmen easy first check-ins, and adds map density at zero partnership cost. Confirm you're comfortable including non-partner landmarks.

## Pilot shape

9 partner locations + optional landmarks, in three zones:
- **Campus:** Carver Museum, Legacy Museum (+ monument/chapel if added)
- **Downtown/near town:** B&B Barbecue, New House of Stewarts, Farmers Market
- **Driving distance:** Airmen Museum (Moton Field), Shady Grove, The Shed(?), Tuskegee Honey(?)

The campus + downtown loop is walkable; the driving-distance ring is where the high point values and event-day shuttle/carpool challenges live.
