# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Primary — Tuskegee University students.** They're on campus or in town, phone in hand, often moving between classes or social plans. They engage in short bursts: checking the map between classes, glancing at the leaderboard before a challenge, scanning for active rewards before heading somewhere to eat. Classification spans Freshman through Graduate; the freshmen are the most likely to discover new places, seniors the most likely to be competitive.

**Secondary — Local business owners, farmers, and museum staff.** They post challenges and stories from a third-party platform (SkyLark). They care about footfall and community engagement more than the app experience itself. Their surface is minimal: post content, see participation.

## Product Purpose

TTN (Tuskegee Trail Network) is a gamified discovery platform that gets students physically moving through Tuskegee. It maps real places — restaurants, farms, museums, historical sites — onto a set of "trails," then uses points, badges, challenges, and a weekly leaderboard to make visiting those places feel meaningful and competitive. Success looks like a student who would never have visited the G.W. Carver Museum making it there because their roommate has a badge they don't.

## Brand Personality

Playful · Competitive · Local

The voice is confident and direct — it knows Tuskegee and speaks to students who live here, not tourists passing through. It celebrates discovery without being breathless about it. It takes the leaderboard seriously but doesn't take itself too seriously.

## Anti-references

- **Pokémon Go / sci-fi game UI**: no neon, no dark map interfaces, no laser grids or futuristic HUD aesthetics. TTN is rooted in a real, historically rich place; the design should feel warm and grounded.
- **Social media feed patterns**: no infinite scroll, no algorithmic content surfacing, no passive consumption loops. Every screen should push the student toward a physical action, not keep them in the app.
- **Generic university portals**: flat navy-and-gray, stock icon sets, bureaucratic hierarchy. TTN is a student product, not an administrative one.
- **Corporate loyalty apps (airline miles / coffee stamps energy)**: transactional and cold. Rewards here are about celebrating exploration of a specific place, not accumulating abstract currency.

## Design Principles

1. **Place over platform.** Every screen should remind the student they're navigating the real world. The map is the soul of the app; the points and badges are the reason to move, not the destination.
2. **Tuskegee-specific, not campus-generic.** The crimson-and-gold palette, the trail names, the stories from farmers and curators — all of it should feel native to this place and its legacy. Nothing interchangeable with another university app.
3. **Earn the game mechanics.** Competition and points only feel good when the progress is visible and the milestones feel real. Every badge, unlocked location, and leaderboard rank should be a small moment of genuine satisfaction.
4. **Short sessions, clear next action.** Students open the app between classes. Every screen should have an obvious "do this next" — a challenge to accept, a location nearby, a trail to continue.
5. **Show the people, not just the places.** Stories from farmers, curators, and owners are the heart of why these places matter. The design should give them space, not bury them under game chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 Level A minimum. All interactive elements must have accessible labels. Color must never be the sole carrier of information (trail types must have icons, not just color). Tap targets minimum 44×44px. The leaderboard and map must be navigable without requiring fine motor precision.
